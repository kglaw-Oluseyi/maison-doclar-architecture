**DI-066 · Evidence tiers formalised: Direct Verification, Operational Verification, Reported Claims.** Founder disposition, 13 July 2026, clarifying the ICAA's standing verification duty (HANDOVER-001 §9 step 6/7; DI-062/DI-064/DI-065 practice). **DI-075 · Constitutional principle: declaration, disposition, and truth are distinct actors' statements and must never share a field.** 13 July 2026. Founder-directed, following research synthesis on RSVP modelling, decline/representation handling, and invitation authority (routed through the knowledge-extraction pipeline per DI-021/024 — see extraction repo `extracted/mps/protocol-doctrine-research.md`). **The principle:** any system field that appears to record "what happened" is frequently conflating three distinct actors' statements — (1) **declaration**: what a party has stated their intent to be (a guest's RSVP response, an inviter's invitation); (2) **disposition**: a decision made about that declaration by a different party with authority over allocation (a host's waitlist decision, a principal's acceptance-via-proxy pending reconfirmation); (3) **truth**: what was actually, observably the case, recorded after the fact by whoever has standing to observe it (gate-recorded attendance, a reconfirmation call). Collapsing these into a single field or status value corrupts all three: it makes the declaration look more certain than it is, hides who actually made the allocation call, and destroys the very discrepancy (declared vs. true) that is the platform's most valuable analytical signal. **Governing status:** constitutional, cited by reference rather than restated — this is the principle behind the RSVP model (DI-076), the decline/representation model, and the invitation-authority model (all three landing via the same extraction pipeline), the way "capability is not authority" governs by reference rather than being re-derived in every specification that depends on it. Status: ratified.

**DI-076 · RSVP model resolved: five-part decomposition, superseding STD-002 G-4's provisional three-state position.** 13 July 2026. Founder-directed, resolving O-1 (STD-002's open item, "final RSVP flag/mode shape — founder ruling on corpus §6") and closing the MPS corpus §6 contradiction (MKC Art. 2/6). **Citation correction, recorded because it matters to constitutional accuracy:** the disposition instructing this entry cited "DI-070, the three-state decomposition" as the ratified position being superseded. DI-070 in this register is unrelated (credential-lifetime scope correction, 13 July 2026). The actual ratified-but-provisional three-state position was never a Decision Register entry — it lives in **STD-002 G-4** ("Three-state RSVP (confirmed/declined/pending)... final shape pins to the founder's §6 corpus ruling"), explicitly marked provisional pending exactly this ruling. This entry supersedes STD-002 G-4's three-state text, not any DI-070 content; STD-002 is updated directly (see below) rather than a nonexistent register entry being marked superseded. **The resolved model, five orthogonal parts, not three:** (1) **Response state** — `not_yet_invited → invited → responded(accepted|declined|tentative) → confirmed`, with `confirmed` distinct from `accepted` (a re-verification event, not the initial reply). (2) **Attendance mode** — in-person/remote/hybrid-undecided, unchanged from the original proposal, orthogonal. (3) **Allocation state** — `offered/waitlisted/released`, the host's capacity decision, NOT a property of the guest's response (correcting the original proposal, which buried this in "flags"). (4) **Engagement state** — `responsive/unresponsive_since(date)/unreachable(channel failure)`, a derived, time-decaying signal computed from contact history, never a human-toggled flag (also correcting the original proposal). (5) **Attendance record** — `arrived/no_show/turned_away/arrived_uninvited`, gate-recorded truth, a separate append-only record that never overwrites response state. **Why the correction happened, not just what changed:** waitlisting is the host's allocation decision, not the guest's declared intent — conflating them in one enum or in "flags" hides who actually made the capacity call and prevents modelling a guest who is accepted-then-waitlisted or waitlisted-then-promoted. Engagement/silence is not a fact a human declares; it is inferred from contact timing and channel health, and treating it as a togglable flag loses the distinction between "not responding" (a social signal, routes to escalation) and "unreachable" (a data-quality problem, routes to channel remediation) — operationally different remediations collapsed into one bit. This is the DI-075 principle (declaration/disposition/truth separation) applied concretely: response state is declaration, allocation state is disposition, the attendance record is truth, and engagement state is a derived signal belonging to none of the three. **Provenance:** research-sourced (mature capacity-management and CRM practice, general and Nigerian-specific), founder-reviewed and endorsed by directive — explicitly not elicited tacit knowledge, per DI-021/024's provenance discipline. Full synthesis staged in `maison-doclar-knowledge-extraction/extracted/mps/protocol-doctrine-research.md`. **STD-002 disposition:** G-4 is updated in place (specifications are living artifacts per HANDOVER-001 §3, unlike frozen historical deliverables) to reference this entry and state the five-part model; O-1 is closed. **Downstream:** this model is a precondition for the new Entourage/Party domain's confirmation-escalation logic (engagement state, tier-sensitive caps) and interacts with the not-yet-modelled "and party" problem, which this entry explicitly does NOT solve (party size remains a numeric field on the acceptance, not an RSVP state, per the research's own recommendation). Status: ratified.
