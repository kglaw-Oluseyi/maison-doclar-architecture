# Knowledge Preservation Matrix — Addendum v1.1
**Deliverable 06A amendments, ratified by ICAA review · 11 July 2026**
This addendum implements the nine amendments and binds them into the matrix. The v1.0 matrix remains the evidence record; this addendum is the classification layer above it. Both feed MKC.

---

## A1 — Knowledge type separation
Every preserved artifact is now one of: **Principle** (always preserved — e.g. "advice degrades to absence, never failure"; "biometrics are deletable by design"), **Algorithm** (usually preserved — allocator engine, phone normalisation, headcount reconciliation, send-time recommendation), **Reference implementation** (preserved only while uniquely valuable — Salle's conversational canvas, remote-view's tenancy model, Assets' worker registry, md-events' hub bridge), or **Disposable implementation** (BullMQ wiring, Hono/Next scaffolding, FFmpeg invocation details, Prisma plumbing — never preserved, only cited). The v1.0 matrix's "PE architecture" entries are re-read accordingly: *worker-registry pattern* is Principle; its FFmpeg processors are Reference; its queue wiring is Disposable.

## A2 — Knowledge confidence classes
**Canonical** (constitutionally true): Relay title taxonomy; the household/engagement taxonomy; three-state RSVP with no-response retirement; event-scoping principle; consent/purge posture. **Validated** (proven in live operation): allocator suite (Alakija-tested); import/dedup engine; snapshot/ack pattern; capsule delivery; MACS order pipeline. **Observed** (works, not stress-proven): Gateway permission model; Salle conversational workflow; remote-view audio participation; hub bridge. **Hypothesis** (designed, unproven): Relay's eight AI micro-capabilities' effectiveness; template-effectiveness scoring; EIDL inference rules. **Historical** (context-bound, verify before reuse): Venari scoring heuristics; Nexus benchmarks and any regulatory seed content; MACS Apps Script reporting conventions.

## A3 — Replacement cost (importance ≠ cost)
Years: MPS protocol corpus; the operational theory in Aperture (it took a live investiture to earn). Months: allocator suite with its test corpus; Nexus simulation engine; snapshot/hub distributed-truth design; Relay channel intelligence; Plavent transaction lifecycle. Weeks: import engine; tenancy model; capsule delivery; Venari scaffold; readiness computation. Days-to-minutes: UI shells, adapters, utilities — regardless of importance. Preservation effort is now prioritised by (importance × replacement cost), which puts the MPS assembly and the Aperture operational theory unambiguously first.

## A4 — Human Knowledge Assets (the founder register)
First-class, highest replacement cost in the company, absent from Git: protocol judgement (when a rule bends for a royal household), host psychology, political etiquette, VIP conflict handling, room-reading heuristics, luxury service instincts, crisis behaviour, vendor trust memory, household memory (who is related to whom, unwritten). Preservation mechanism: structured elicitation into MPS (rules with confidence "Canonical-by-founder"), Event Intelligence lesson capture (crisis behaviour becomes lessons), Vendor Intelligence (trust memory becomes scored edges), and Decision Intelligence (judgement becomes recorded rationale). This register is a standing MKC obligation, not a one-time interview.

## A5 — Architectural Fossils register
Historical artifacts that encode decisions: `retire_rsvp_no_response` (constitutional: no-response is an escalation flag, not a state); `verify-allocator-suggestion-event-scope.ts` (contamination happened; the check is law); `guest_list_import_lock` (imports and allocation must not race); `print_consent_report_dispatch` (consent gates physical artifacts); `prompt58_allocator_rebuild` (the allocator earned a rebuild — v1 limits are known); facegate's `purge-biometrics` endpoint (deletability as design); Gateway's one-time-reveal keys (secrets shown once). Fossils are preserved with their *why* attached; a fossil without its story is just old code.

## A6 — Negative Knowledge register
Do-not-repeat, with evidence: **Gen-2 federation at sole-operator scale** (twenty services exceeded operational capacity; evidence: revealed re-consolidation into Aperture). **Cross-event contamination** (evidence: the verify script and its migration context). **No-response as an RSVP state** (evidence: its retirement migration). **Mandatory AI proxy** (evidence: fifteen bypasses; governance replaces execution). **Per-app guest models** (evidence: eight divergent copies and the data bugs they bred). **Provider lock-in without an exit** (evidence: the Cloudinary→B2 migration effort). **Unversioned architecture** (evidence: three conflicting "source of truth" documents; the Event OS spec living only in chat history). MKC preserves failures with the same rigor as successes.
